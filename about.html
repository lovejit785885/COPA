    • What are ITI Courses?

  • Industrial Training Institute courses or the ITI courses, are vocational training programs. These programs are primarily targeted to equip students with the technical know-how and practical skills they need to succeed in particular trades.
  • ITI courses are career-focused and emphasize instructing students in a range of technical disciplines.
  • They provide a broad range of specializations, including

    • ITI Admission: Eligibility Criteria

    Only students who have completed their studies to the level of 10 or 10+2 are eligible to enroll in ITI courses. Besides this, a few other prerequisites are -

  • Entrance Exam:

    Although it's not always required, several states and institutions hold entrance exams to admit students to ITI programs.

  • Education Requirement:

    The majority of ITI courses call for a pass in the 10th standard from a recognized board. Also, students might need at least 40% to 50% marks in 10+2 to be eligible to study ITI.

    • ITI Admission: Process

  • Admission to the top ITI programs after 12th grade varies by state.
  • Most states offer admission based on 10+2 marks, while some states may need an entrance exam.
  • For entrance-based admission, there will be 100 multiple-choice questions that must be solved in three hours

    • For entrance-based admission, there will be 100 multiple-choice questions that must be solved in three hours

  • Long-Term Courses :

    These are longer, more in-depth courses that often last between one and two years. They seek to give a thorough understanding of a particular trade. Courses for electricians, fitters, turners, machinists, diesel mechanics, welders, and other trades are a few examples

  • Short-term Courses:

    These are programs that last between six months and a year. They are tailored to equip students with a particular set of skills.

    • ITI Courses: Skills Required

  • ITI offers technical knowledge; hence, students must place more emphasis on disciplines like mathematics, physics, chemistry, and engineering principles.
  • Students will learn about soft skills and abilities, such as acquiring a certain language, that is helpful in the industry in other non-technical courses.

    • Jobs and Scope After ITI Courses

  • Students after finishing their ITI courses may have access to a variety of work prospects. Also, they may be able to work in Government sector, Private industry, Industrial factories, and Self-employment businesses
  • Any discipline in ITI courses is demanding since they assist students in acquiring the technical skills necessary to succeed in the modern workplace. ITI-skilled people are needed by both government and private industries to operate in sectors including transportation, manufacturing, construction, hospitality, and aviation, among others.
  • Students can explore careers as machine operators, assistant engineers, draughtsmen, technicians, etc. Additionally, the ITI program can allow students to pursue advanced courses at the bachelor's and diploma levels.

    ITI Courses Feess

    Depending on the course and state of the candidate fees for ITI courses may vary. Furthermore, costs also differ from college to college. In comparison to private ITI colleges, government-run ITI colleges have lower tuition fees.

  • Average fees for government ITI colleges: INR 1,000 to INR 20,000
  • Fees for private ITI colleges: INR 5,000 to INR 1,000,000
